1. Purpose of SDLC

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a structured process for planning, building, testing, and deploying software. It ensures the software aligns with business goals, is cost-effective, secure, and scalable.

2. Why SDLC Matters

  • Establishes accountability
  • Encourages early risk identification
  • Promotes cross-functional collaboration
  • Embeds quality and security into the workflow

4. SDLC Models

Model Use Case Risk
Waterfall Fixed-scope projects Little flexibility for change
Agile Fast-changing requirements Needs constant collaboration
Scrum Time-boxed sprints Heavily reliant on ceremonies
Spiral Risk-driven projects Complex and expensive
V-Model Critical software (e.g. medical, aerospace) Heavy test process overhead
Big Bang Experimental prototypes Unpredictable, risky

5. Modern SDLC = Shift-Left Thinking

Security and testing are no longer "end-of-pipeline" concerns. Dev teams now embrace Shift Left:

  • Integrate testing early
  • Introduce security early
  • Automate everything with CI/CD

This naturally leads to DevSecOps: embedding security into every phase of the SDLC.

6. Summary for Non-Technical Stakeholders

Think of SDLC like building a smart city:
  • Plan roads, power, buildings (Planning, Requirements)
  • Design the layout and safety features (Design)
  • Construct with quality materials (Development)
  • Test for safety and regulations (Testing)
  • Open for public use (Deployment)
  • Maintain and improve over time (Maintenance)
